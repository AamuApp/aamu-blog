<p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Aamu.app Helpdesk is the customer-support part of the Aamu workspace. It brings customer email, website live chat, tickets, internal comments, tasks, and AI-assisted support into the same project-based system.</p><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">This overview assumes you have already seen the setup articles for <a target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" href="https://aamu.app/blog/posts/setting-up-helpdesk-in-aamuapp-part-1-emails/">Helpdesk email</a> and <a target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" href="https://aamu.app/blog/posts/setting-up-helpdesk-in-aamuapp-part-2-livechat/">Helpdesk Livechat</a>. Here we focus on how the Helpdesk is used day to day.</p><h2 xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">The Helpdesk model</h2><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Aamu Helpdesk is organized by project. A project can have its own Helpdesk users, shared support email address, live chat widget, AI settings, and knowledge sources.</p><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Support can arrive through several routes:</p><ul xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"><li><p>customer email to the Helpdesk mailbox,</p></li><li><p>public Livechat from your website,</p></li><li><p>email fallback from the Livechat widget,</p></li><li><p>manual tickets created by the team, and</p></li><li><p>integrations through the Helpdesk API.</p></li></ul><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">The result is the same: the team works with tickets in Aamu.</p><h2 xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Ticket views</h2><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">The Helpdesk list is split into practical views so the team can focus on the right queue.</p><ul xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"><li><p><strong>Unseen</strong> shows new tickets the user has not seen yet.</p></li><li><p><strong>Open</strong> shows active tickets.</p></li><li><p><strong>Starred</strong> shows tickets marked as important.</p></li><li><p><strong>Closed</strong> shows finished tickets.</p></li><li><p><strong>Spam</strong> separates unwanted messages.</p></li><li><p><strong>Archived</strong> keeps old tickets out of the active queue.</p></li></ul><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">You can work inside one Helpdesk project or use an all-projects view when you need to scan support across the team.</p><h2 xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Inside a ticket</h2><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">A ticket contains the customer message and the support workflow around it. Depending on the source, it can include the original email body, email attachments, a connected live chat, customer details, comments, internal comments, tags, assignment, status, feedback, and links to follow-up work.</p><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Common ticket actions include:</p><ul xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"><li><p>assigning the ticket to a support user,</p></li><li><p>closing or reopening the ticket,</p></li><li><p>closing and marking the issue as resolved,</p></li><li><p>starring important tickets,</p></li><li><p>tagging tickets for later search,</p></li><li><p>replying to the customer,</p></li><li><p>leaving internal comments, and</p></li><li><p>creating a task from the ticket.</p></li></ul><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">This keeps support work visible. A ticket is not only a message: it is the record of what happened, who is responsible, and what still needs to be done.</p><h2 xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Email tickets</h2><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">When a customer writes to the configured Helpdesk address, Aamu turns the message into a ticket. Replies from Aamu go back through the configured Helpdesk email route, so the customer sees the shared support address rather than a random personal mailbox.</p><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Email tickets can show the original email, attachments, and customer information. For security and readability, images can be controlled separately, and obvious spam can be marked as spam.</p><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">This is the best support route for longer, asynchronous conversations where the customer does not need an immediate answer.</p><h2 xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Livechat tickets</h2><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Aamu Livechat lets visitors contact you while they are still on your website. Chats are connected to the Helpdesk project, and the team can answer from Aamu.</p><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Livechat is useful when timing matters: sales questions, onboarding friction, product questions, or support moments where the visitor is deciding what to do next.</p><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">When a chat leads to follow-up work, keep the customer conversation in Helpdesk and create a task for the internal work. That keeps the customer-facing thread and the team's execution connected.</p><h2 xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Internal comments</h2><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Helpdesk supports internal comments separately from customer replies. Use internal comments for notes the customer should not see: diagnosis, assignment context, escalation details, links to private docs, or reminders for another team member.</p><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">This is especially useful when several people collaborate on one ticket. The public customer conversation stays clean, while the internal reasoning stays attached to the ticket.</p><h2 xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Tasks from support</h2><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Many support tickets are really the beginning of work. A customer reports a bug, asks for a change, needs onboarding help, or reveals a process issue. In Aamu, you can create a task from a Helpdesk ticket.</p><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">The practical pattern is:</p><ol xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"><li><p>Handle the customer conversation in Helpdesk.</p></li><li><p>Create a task for the internal follow-up.</p></li><li><p>Assign the task to the right person or project.</p></li><li><p>Return to the ticket when there is something useful to tell the customer.</p></li></ol><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">This avoids the common support problem where customer requests disappear into chat messages or private notes.</p><h2 xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Helpdesk AI and Team Brain</h2><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Aamu Helpdesk can use AI knowledge sources for ticket and email reply drafts. The important word is <strong>drafts</strong>: for email and Helpdesk tickets, AI prepares text in the normal reply editor, and a human decides what to send.</p><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Team admins can configure Helpdesk AI sources in project settings. Current source types include:</p><ul xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"><li><p>page URLs,</p></li><li><p>sitemap URLs,</p></li><li><p>manual FAQ snippets, and</p></li><li><p>Aamu Docs.</p></li></ul><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">The sources are indexed into Team Brain. The team can reindex sources and preview retrieval with real customer questions before relying on AI-generated drafts.</p><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">There is also a setting to use resolved tickets as AI knowledge sources. Use it carefully: resolved tickets can be useful examples, but only if the old answers are still correct.</p><h2 xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Livechat AI</h2><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Livechat has a separate AI setting: <strong>Allow AI to answer live chat messages</strong>. When enabled, the public widget can open in chat mode and AI can answer from Team Brain knowledge sources.</p><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">This is more immediate than a reply draft. A live chat answer may be shown directly to a visitor, so the team should prepare the knowledge sources and escalation rules first.</p><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Use AI Livechat for common, safe, well-documented questions. Use human support for account-specific, sensitive, contractual, billing, security, or uncertain cases.</p><h2 xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Search and tags</h2><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Tickets are searchable, and tags help make support history easier to find. Use tags for product areas, customer types, incident categories, priority, or recurring themes.</p><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">The value of tags grows over time. A tag like <code>billing</code>, <code>onboarding</code>, <code>bug-report</code>, or <code>refund</code> can make it much easier to review patterns later.</p><h2 xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Helpdesk API</h2><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Aamu exposes Helpdesk through the API for integrations and automation. The core ticket endpoints include:</p><pre xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"><code class="language-plaintext">GET    /api/v1/helpdesk/tickets/
GET    /api/v1/helpdesk/tickets/{id}
GET    /api/v1/helpdesk/actors/{actor}
PUT    /api/v1/helpdesk/tickets/{id}/reply-draft
DELETE /api/v1/helpdesk/tickets/{id}/reply-draft
POST   /api/v1/helpdesk/tickets/{id}/reply-draft/generate
POST   /api/v1/helpdesk/tickets/{id}/reply-draft/send</code></pre><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">The API is designed around human-in-the-loop support automation. Integrations can list tickets, find unanswered tickets, write or generate reply drafts, and send a draft through an explicit send command.</p><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">When Team Brain is used for draft generation, the API key needs both Helpdesk write scope and Team Brain read scope for the project.</p><h2 xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">When Helpdesk works best</h2><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Aamu Helpdesk works best when customer support is connected to the rest of the workspace. A ticket can create a task. A repeated answer can become a Doc. A Doc can become Team Brain knowledge. A live chat can become a ticket. An email reply can stay inside the shared support queue.</p><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">The goal is not just to answer messages. The goal is to keep customer communication, internal context, and follow-up work in one place.</p>
