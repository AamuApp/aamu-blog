<p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Aamu.app can work as a Calendly alternative for teams that want booking links, tasks, and video meetings in the same workspace. Instead of using a separate scheduling tool and then connecting it back to your actual work, Aamu creates the booking as part of the project workflow.</p><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">The basic idea is simple: you configure an event booking page, share the booking link, and when someone books a time Aamu creates the related work item for you. If the event type uses video, Aamu can also create the meeting.</p><h2 xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">What Aamu replaces</h2><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Calendly is useful because it removes the back-and-forth of scheduling. Aamu's Event Booking feature is useful for the same reason, but it also connects the booking to the rest of your workspace.</p><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">A booking can become:</p><ul xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"><li><p>a task in the project,</p></li><li><p>a scheduled event your team can track,</p></li><li><p>a video meeting when the event type calls for it, and</p></li><li><p>a piece of work that can live near docs, comments, tasks, and follow-up.</p></li></ul><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">That is the main difference. Aamu is not only a booking page. It is a workspace where the booking creates something the team can continue working with.</p><h2 xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Where to configure Event Booking</h2><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Open the project settings and go to the Event Booking settings under Tasks. This is where you configure the public booking page for the project.</p><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">The exact settings may vary by workspace, but the important options are the same kind of things you would expect from a scheduling tool:</p><ul xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"><li><p>the booking link,</p></li><li><p>the event type or meeting type,</p></li><li><p>available days and times,</p></li><li><p>duration or time slots,</p></li><li><p>how unavailable times are shown, and</p></li><li><p>whether the booking should create a video meeting.</p></li></ul><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Once the settings are ready, copy the event booking link and share it with people who should be able to book time with you.</p><h2 xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">The booking page</h2><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">The public booking page shows available times in the visitor's timezone. That matters because scheduling problems are often timezone problems in disguise.</p><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">The visitor chooses a suitable time, enters contact details, and confirms the booking. The page can show unavailable days or times as disabled, so the person booking does not need to ask which slots are possible.</p><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">For a simple meeting workflow, this is enough: share the link, let the visitor choose a time, and let Aamu create the work item.</p><h2 xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">What happens after someone books?</h2><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">After a booking is confirmed, Aamu creates a task. This is intentional. Tasks are the natural place to track upcoming work in Aamu: they can have details, status, comments, assignees, dates, and follow-up.</p><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">If the booking is a video meeting, Aamu can also create the meeting and connect it to the task. That means the booking is not floating somewhere outside the workspace. It is attached to the place where the team can prepare for it and act on it afterwards.</p><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">You can then use the task like any other Aamu task:</p><ul xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"><li><p>add notes before the call,</p></li><li><p>assign someone to handle it,</p></li><li><p>comment internally,</p></li><li><p>link related docs or customer information,</p></li><li><p>create follow-up tasks, or</p></li><li><p>sync task timing to an external calendar when your setup supports it.</p></li></ul><h2 xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Why connect booking to tasks?</h2><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">A booking is usually not just a calendar entry. It often represents real work: a sales call, onboarding session, support meeting, consultation, interview, demo, or internal planning conversation.</p><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">If the booking only lives in a scheduling app, the team still has to move the context somewhere else. In Aamu, the booking becomes a task, so follow-up has a natural home.</p><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">That matters for small teams especially. You may not want a separate booking tool, separate meeting tool, separate task manager, and separate follow-up process. Aamu keeps those pieces closer together.</p><h2 xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">When Aamu is a good Calendly alternative</h2><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Aamu Event Booking is a good fit when you want scheduling to connect to team work. For example:</p><ul xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"><li><p>customer calls that need follow-up tasks,</p></li><li><p>product demos that should create an internal work item,</p></li><li><p>support calls where notes and next steps matter,</p></li><li><p>consultation slots connected to a project,</p></li><li><p>interviews or onboarding meetings, and</p></li><li><p>video meetings that should be created automatically.</p></li></ul><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">If all you need is a standalone scheduling page with no connection to the rest of your work, a dedicated scheduling tool may be enough. If you want the booking to become part of the project workflow, Aamu makes more sense.</p><h2 xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">A practical setup</h2><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">A simple setup can look like this:</p><ol xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"><li><p>Open the project settings.</p></li><li><p>Go to Tasks and Event Booking.</p></li><li><p>Configure the event type, duration, availability, and meeting behavior.</p></li><li><p>Copy the public booking link.</p></li><li><p>Share the link on your website, in email, or with a specific customer.</p></li><li><p>When someone books, review the created task in Aamu.</p></li><li><p>Use the task and meeting for preparation, the call itself, and follow-up.</p></li></ol><h2 xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Tips</h2><ul xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"><li><p>Use a clear event name, such as "15-minute intro call" or "Product demo".</p></li><li><p>Keep the duration realistic. Shorter booking slots work well for quick calls; longer ones are better for demos and onboarding.</p></li><li><p>Add enough buffer in your availability so bookings do not create a packed calendar.</p></li><li><p>If the meeting needs preparation, use the created task to collect notes before the call.</p></li><li><p>If the booking often leads to follow-up work, create those follow-up tasks directly from the original task.</p></li></ul><h2 xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">In summary</h2><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">Aamu.app can replace a separate scheduling tool when booking is part of a larger workflow. The booking link lets people choose a time, Aamu creates a task, and video meetings can be created as part of the same flow.</p><p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">The value is not only that someone can book time with you. The value is that the booking lands where the work already happens.</p>
